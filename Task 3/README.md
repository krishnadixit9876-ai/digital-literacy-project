## 📝 Reflection (150–200 words)

This task introduced me to two very different but equally important digital platforms. HackerRank is a competitive programming platform widely used by companies to evaluate candidates during hiring. Solving even a basic problem on it helped me understand the interface — how to read problem statements, write and test code, and submit solutions. The experience made me want to solve more problems and build a visible problem-solving track record.

The Google Form exercise was surprisingly practical. Creating a survey requires you to think clearly about what information you want, how to phrase neutral questions, and which question format (multiple choice, checkbox, scale) best captures honest answers. These are skills directly applicable to research projects, user feedback collection, and even team surveys in a professional setting.

Both platforms are free, widely recognised, and used by professionals globally. Knowing how to navigate them gives me a real advantage — it is not just about completing an assignment, it is about becoming familiar with tools I will use throughout my career.

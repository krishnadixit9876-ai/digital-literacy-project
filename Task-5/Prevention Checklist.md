# 🛡️ UPI Fraud Prevention Checklist

Use this checklist to protect yourself from UPI and online payment fraud.

---

## ✅ 10-Point Prevention Checklist

- [ ] **1. Never share your UPI PIN with anyone**  
  Your UPI PIN is like your ATM PIN. No bank, payment app, or customer care representative will ever ask for it. Anyone who does is a scammer — end the conversation immediately.

- [ ] **2. Verify before you click**  
  Never click links sent via WhatsApp, SMS, or email that claim to be from your bank or UPI app. Always open your bank's app or website directly by typing the official URL.

- [ ] **3. Double-check the recipient before confirming payment**  
  Before hitting "Pay", verify the recipient's name displayed by the UPI app. If the name doesn't match or seems odd, stop and verify independently.

- [ ] **4. Ignore "Request Money" scams**  
  Scammers send UPI collect requests disguised as "refunds" or "cashbacks." Accepting a collect request means **you are paying them**, not the other way around. Always read the direction of money flow.

- [ ] **5. Do not scan QR codes from unknown sources**  
  Fraudsters share QR codes that initiate payment from your account. A QR code from a stranger online should never be scanned.

- [ ] **6. Enable transaction alerts and daily limits**  
  Set up SMS and email alerts for every UPI transaction. Enable daily transaction limits in your UPI app to cap potential losses.

- [ ] **7. Use official apps only**  
  Download UPI apps (BHIM, PhonePe, GPay, Paytm) only from the official Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Avoid APKs shared via links.

- [ ] **8. Report fraud immediately — don't wait**  
  If fraud occurs, call **1930** (National Cyber Helpline) within minutes. Speed matters — banks can freeze pending transactions if reported fast enough.

- [ ] **9. File a formal complaint at cybercrime.gov.in**  
  Visit [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) to file a written complaint. Keep your transaction IDs, screenshots, and contact details ready.

- [ ] **10. Talk about it — awareness is protection**  
  Most UPI fraud victims are first-time targets. Share this checklist with family members, especially those less familiar with digital payments. Elderly relatives are frequently targeted.

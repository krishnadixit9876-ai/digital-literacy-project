### Social Media Do's and Don'ts

### 5 DO's

1. **Keep your profile professional** — Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and a bio that reflects your skills and goals.
2. **Share relevant content** — Post articles, projects, or achievements that are related to your field of study or career interest.
3. **Engage respectfully** — Comment and reply thoughtfully; your online tone reflects your character.
4. **Update your profiles regularly** — Add new skills, certifications, or projects as you complete them.
5. **Use privacy settings wisely** — Know who can see your posts; separate personal and professional content where needed.

###  5 DON'Ts

1. **Don't post in anger or frustration** — Screenshots last forever; think before you type.
2. **Don't share personal information publicly** — Phone number, address, or ID details should never be on public profiles.
3. **Don't badmouth institutions, professors, or employers** — Even indirectly, this can damage your reputation permanently.
4. **Don't ignore connection requests from unknown people without checking** — Many fake profiles exist to harvest data.
5. **Don't use informal language or slang on professional platforms** — LinkedIn is not WhatsApp; your communication style matters.

## 📝 Reflection 

Before this task, I rarely thought about the difference between sending a casual message and writing a professional email. This task made that gap very clear. A professional email has a structure — subject line, salutation, clear body, and sign-off — and every part matters. The tone must be respectful but not overly formal; clear but not blunt.

Writing the internship email was particularly useful because I realised I had never actually written one before. The social media checklist reinforced something important: everything you post is part of your public identity. Recruiters do check social media. The Do's and Don'ts are not rules for their own sake — they are habits that protect your reputation and open doors rather than close them.
